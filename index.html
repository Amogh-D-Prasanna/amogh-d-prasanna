Origin

The day Turing met Feynman (and I was in the room)

It was my second year of undergrad. The exam said Theory of Computation: Turing machines, decidability, the mathematical spine of everything that computes, what stayed with me wasn't the grade. It was the questions...

That same day, I made a decision: I would go deeper into the very field that challenged me most, to understand what lies beyond it.

That stubbornness carried me from classical computation into the quantum world, where Turing's limits meet Feynman's dreams. Currently, I'm a Research Assistant at UMD, working and researching on Quantum Information Processing and Theory of Computation. The subject that once humbled me became the subject I now push forward.
